## Section 1: Introduction to Nursing Application: Nutrition and Immunity

Nutrition plays a large role in the health of those who are immunocompromised. This concept explores nutrition and immunity within application activities using the nursing process and clinical judgment.

As you complete these application activities, you will gain the knowledge and skills needed to:

- Utilize prior knowledge of theories and principles of nutrition and immunity.
- Utilize prior knowledge of theories and principles of nursing while implementing the nursing process.
- Demonstrate clinical decision-making as it pertains to the concept.

## Section 3: Explore: Case Scenario: Cancer

Cory (pronouns: he/him/his) is a 25-year-old male who works at a local construction company as a heavy equipment operator. He loves his job and the team he works with. He recently got engaged to his long-time girlfriend who he considers the love of his life. Money is tight because they are saving money to buy a house and have a beautiful wedding.

Cory's friends are mainly his co-workers as he is not very close with his family. He doesn't really have a best friend but will go out with the guys after work. He regards his fiancée's family as his.

One evening, Cory's fiancée mentioned to him that there was a lump on his testicle, and he should see a healthcare provider. He reassured her that it was nothing and he was fine. A month later, he noticed that it was larger and made an appointment. He didn't tell anyone because he didn't want them to be worried.

An ultrasound revealed that it was a solid mass, and blood tumor marker tests were positive for testicular cancer. The healthcare provider recommended that Cory undergo a radical inguinal orchidectomy, removing the testicle. They would stage the cancer, and he may need chemotherapy. Cory was devastated but scheduled the surgery. The healthcare provider discussed the importance of good nutrition prior to surgery.

That night he told his fiancée.

## Section 6: Explore: Coping With Diagnosis

After the surgery, Cory was diagnosed with Stage II testicular cancer. Tests also found that the cancer had spread to the abdominal lymph nodes, so the healthcare provider discussed retroperitoneal lymph node dissection. It is a major surgery that would affect fertility. He also began four cycles of chemotherapy.

Cory felt embarrassed about having only one testicle and now, with finding out that it would be more difficult to have children, he became depressed. The chemotherapy made him tired and he lost his appetite for food. He began to lose weight. His fiancée was heartbroken by the news. Her dream was to have a large family. Although the healthcare provider assured them both that they could have children by other means, it was too much for her. She broke off her engagement and left Cory.

He felt like he wasn't worth anything. Without any support from work, he had thoughts of giving up and stopping treatment. As a result, he barely ate and didn't take care of himself.

---
## Section 9: Explore: Cancer and Decreased Nutrition

Due to his decrease in weight, the healthcare provider discussed the situation with Cory. Cory needs to be healthier in order to proceed with the retroperitoneal lymph node dissection. The provider suggested that Cory see a counselor and a dietitian. The counselor would help Cory with coping skills, while the dietitian would help with methods to increase calorie intake.

The provider also discussed ways to help with the anorexia and any taste abnormalities that Cory had from the chemotherapy. Another option discussed was to use enteral nutrition to provide nutrition. Cory would spend a few days in the hospital to regain nutrition and energy.

The provider stressed to Cory that he has a very good chance of survival if the dissection is completed and chemotherapy is finished. The side effects are temporary and most resolved 3–4 weeks after treatment. Cory agreed to the enteral nutrition. In the hospital, he realized that he needed to fight the cancer and get his life back. Some of his co-workers came to visit and that inspired Cory. They offered to help him out so he could get the surgery and recover.

## Section 10: Explore: Case Scenario: Human Immunodeficiency Virus

Now, consider the same client, but replace cancer with human immunodeficiency virus (HIV).

Cory started to get sick a lot and was slowly losing weight. Cory found out that he contracted HIV from a previous partner. His fiancée was devastated and left him. Because he was so weak, he lost his job. His life was falling apart, and he wanted to give up.

At first, Cory refused treatment due to his depressed mood. The healthcare provider warned him that if he did not start treatment, he would put himself at risk for acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS). Once he had this wasting syndrome, the chance of dying is high. So, Cory started on antiretrovirals. He noticed side effects like nausea and vomiting. He had mouth sores that made it difficult to eat. Since starting the medications, Cory had to change his lifestyle to live with these side effects and a suppressed immune system.
