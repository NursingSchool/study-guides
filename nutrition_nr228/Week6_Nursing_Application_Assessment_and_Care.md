## Section 1: Introduction to Nursing Application: Assessment and Care

Clinical decision making is important in identifying actual and potential health risks and providing interventions that promote health. The following application activities will focus on the nursing process and clinical judgment as it pertains to nutritional assessment and care.

If you are not yet comfortable applying this knowledge, please return to the nutritional assessment and care concepts to review prior to completing this activity.

As you complete this learning activity, you will gain the knowledge and skills needed to:

- Utilize prior knowledge of theories and principles of nursing while implementing the nursing process.
- Recognize life span influences that impact nutrition and care.
- Demonstrate clinical decision making as it pertains to the concept.

**Nursing Process / Clinical Judgment (image transcript)**

- **Assessment (Recognize Cues):** Can students identify the most important information in a client scenario? — Recognize signs and symptoms; recognize normal vs. abnormal; identify history of.
- **Analysis (Analyze Cues, Prioritize Hypothesis):** Can students distinguish the most important information? — Connection between pathophysiology and client presentation; use findings/observations to determine client needs. Can students synthesize important information to determine client needs? — Prioritize client needs (likelihood, risk, etc.).
- **Planning (Generate Solutions):** Can students develop possible care options that align with client needs? — Things to address; things to avoid.
- **Implementation (Take Action):** Can students identify and perform the appropriate clinical actions? — Potential steps vs. appropriate steps; is potential action indicated, nonessential or contraindicated. Actions may be: request an order; administer a treatment or medication; perform (skill); document and/or communicate.
- **Evaluation (Evaluate Outcomes):** Can students evaluate outcomes and determine the effectiveness of actions they have deemed appropriate? — What findings indicate improvement or worsening of a condition?

## Section 3: Explore: Nutrition Scenario

Let's take a look at a scenario involving nutrition. To begin, review Ed Wheeler's information below.

### Client Information

- **Name:** Ed Wheeler (pronouns: he/him/his)
- **Age:** 67
- **Allergies:** Lactose Intolerant
- **Status:** Married, wife—Stella
- **Weight:** 172 lbs.
- **Vital Signs:** BP 142/90; Pulse 76; Respiratory rate 12; Temperature 99 °F (37.2 °C)
- **Past Medical History:** coronary artery disease
- **Medications:** aspirin 81 mg daily, lisinopril 5 mg daily, multivitamin

**Note:** Mr. Wheeler presents today with worsening edema in the legs and ankles. Edema is +2 pitting. Weight has increased 7 pounds in last month. He complains of fatigue and wants to "get more energy to spend time with family." Lungs are clear. Diagnostics revealed mild right-sided heart failure.

**Recommendations:** Increase lisinopril to 10 mg daily. Start furosemide 20 mg. Referred for dietitian consult.

## Section 6: Explore: Special Diets and Restrictions

Ed meets with a registered dietitian and reviews the Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension (DASH) diet recommended by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute for lowering blood pressure. The dietitian also discusses a low sodium diet. They give him client education materials on **angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors** and **diuretics** since both can alter potassium levels in the body. Ed also talked about his lactose intolerance and the dietitian gives him some substitutions.

### Tab: Dairy-Free Diet

Suggested replacements for dairy products include:

| Dairy Product | Replacement |
| --- | --- |
| cow's milk | calcium-fortified soy, almond, hemp, or rice milk |
| yogurt | soy or non-dairy yogurt |
| cheese | soy or nut-based cheese |
| ricotta cheese | making your own (home-made) tofu |
| ice cream | sorbet or frozen desserts from soy, coconut, or rice milk |
| cream cheese | soy-based cream cheese |
| butter or margarine | non-dairy margarine |
| sour cream | non-dairy sour cream |
| cream (in coffee) | soy cream, coconut cream, non-dairy milks |

### Tab: DASH Diet

- fruits
- vegetables
- whole grains
- low-fat dairy
- lean meats
- nuts, seeds, legumes
- low-fat oils
- limited sugars

### Tab: Low-Sodium Diet

Clients should be advised to **avoid** the following when on a low-sodium diet:

- canned or processed fruits, vegetables, meats and fish
- fast food
- snack foods
- sauces like soy, teriyaki, barbecue, and steak
- processed cheese

## Section 9: Explore: Food Safety

Ed attended a family picnic celebrating his granddaughter's engagement. It started at 2:00 p.m. Everyone bought a dish to pass, and the foods were set on the picnic tables upon arrival. At 3:30 p.m., Ed's son started to grill steak, chicken, and vegetables. Ed asked his son if he knew when the meat and vegetables would be ready to eat. His son said he had a thermometer to check the meat's temperature. Ed asked his son not to add too much salt to the meat since he is watching his sodium intake.

Ed enjoyed talking with his family all afternoon and drinking iced tea. All the beverages were kept on ice since it was a warm day. It was nice to see the whole family together when they sat down to eat at 4:30 p.m. They celebrated until sunset.

## Section 10: Explore: Food-Borne Illness

The next morning, Ed woke up with stomach cramps and diarrhea. He thought it would go away but by the afternoon, he had a 100.8 °F (38 °C) fever and the diarrhea was worse. Because he is on a diuretic, Ed was concerned about dehydration and went to urgent care. A stool sample was taken, and he was diagnosed with salmonella food poisoning.

The healthcare provider reported the incident to public health, so they can trace the cause. Ed was placed on loperamide, ciprofloxacin, and told to drink plenty of fluids. He recovered in 5 days.
